[Media reports] Inventec Group expands precision medicine service territory and joins hands with Global Life to launch health promotion spillover insurance policy

Inventec Group Inventec Appliances's Good Future and Global Life announced a cross-industry cooperation to jointly launch spillover insurance policies that are in line with health promotion. Policyholders actively undergo cancer screening through Good Future's "circulating tumor cells test" (Circulating Tumor Cell test, hereinafter referred to as CTC test)...

Good Future General Manager Zeng Qingan (second from left) and Global Life Deputy General Manager Zheng Zhongan (second from right).

Inventec Group Inventec Appliances's Good Future and Global Life announced a cross-industry cooperation to jointly launch spillover insurance policies that are in line with health promotion. Policyholders can proactively undergo cancer screening through Good Future's "circulating tumor cells test" (Circulating Tumor Cell test, hereinafter referred to as CTC test), so that they can know as early as possible whether there is a risk of cancer, so as to achieve the purpose of early detection and early treatment, thereby achieving the benefit of promoting health.

According to the Ministry of Health and Welfare’s 110-year cause-of-death statistics in China, malignant tumors, commonly known as cancer, still rank first among the top ten causes of death in terms of mortality. Therefore, the National Health Department calls on the Chinese people: Cancer has no obvious symptoms in the early stages and is difficult to detect. Only early detection through cancer screening and early treatment can avoid greater subsequent medical expenses. With the rapid development of science and technology, there are more and more cancer screening tools and they are becoming easier. This time Good Future cooperates with Global Life Insurance. Through Good Future's exclusive negative screening technology, early screening of circulating tumor cells can be completed with a simple blood collection, which can monitor the risk of cancer and assist in early treatment and tracking to achieve the purpose of health promotion and cancer prevention.

Zeng Qing'an, general manager of Good Future and head of the electronic medical business of Inventec Appliances, said that when cancer cells enter the bloodstream from the growth site of primary tumors or metastatic tumors, they are called circulating tumor cells (CTCs). These CTCs will circulate throughout the body with the blood. At this time, if the immune cells in the body cannot recognize CTCs and destroy them, CTCs may take the opportunity to invade other organs, leading to the growth of metastatic tumors. It took Good Future five years to develop the fully automated circulating tumor cells detection system using exclusive negative screening technology. After receiving recognition from the 17th National Innovation Award in 2020, it has actively expanded the cancer detection market at home and abroad. This cooperation with Global Life to launch health promotion spillover policy products is a major milestone and affirmation for Good Future. It is hoped that CTC testing services will be more popular in the future and benefit more people.

Zheng Zhongan, senior deputy general manager of Global Life, said that through professional CTC testing, policyholders can not only enhance their awareness of self-care, but also learn whether they are at risk of cancer through the test results. For policyholders with test results showing a risk of cancer, early detection and early treatment can also be achieved. Therefore, in order to enhance policyholders’ awareness of self-care, Global Life provides all policies that meet the health promotion conditions designed by this policy. Because it helps reduce the risk of future policy claims, you can enjoy the spillover mechanism provided by this product, allowing policyholders to actively respond to the health promotion conditions designed by the product while planning for family protection, and achieve the purpose of personal health promotion and cancer prevention.
