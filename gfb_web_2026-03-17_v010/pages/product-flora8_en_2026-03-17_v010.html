Why do women and children need to pay attention to bacterial balance?

Most immune cells in the human body are distributed in the intestines, and the daily protection of women and children is highly related to the health of the bacteria. When the intestinal flora is imbalanced, it not only affects digestion, but also affects physical fitness and mucosal protection.

Flora8 specifically complements the Lactobacillus and Bifidobacterium genera, and is positioned as a product in the most commonly mentioned application scenarios of women, infants and children to assist in daily bacterial phase maintenance and physical adjustment.

Bifidobacterium group

Common bacterial species in early childhood colonization and bacterial balance

  • Bifidobacterium longum subsp. infantis: considered to be one of the early colonizers of the infant intestinal tract.
  • Bifidobacterium breve: Commonly found in the intestines of breastfed infants, it can ferment sugars.
  • Bifidobacterium bifidum: Common in young, healthy guts and associated with B vitamin production.
  • Bifidobacterium animalis subsp. lactis: resistant to gastric acid and bile salts, often used in bacterial phase maintenance formulas.
Lactobacillus group

Common bacterial species for physical adjustment and digestive tract maintenance

  • Lactobacillus paracasei: a common LP bacteria in Taiwan, commonly used in daily formula adjustments.
  • Lactobacillus rhamnosus: acid-resistant and bile salt-resistant, related to the maintenance of digestive tract function.
  • Lactobacillus acidophilus: often colonizes the small intestine and is related to lactose metabolism and bacterial balance.
  • Lactobacillus plantarum: survives longer in the stomach and can help maintain intestinal bacteria.
